Aluminum Bottle Campaign

A fun, educational poster campaign encouraging kids to choose aluminum water bottles over one use plastic. My idea behind it was to play with the fun energy that kids naturally respond to, while still delivering a clear environmental message. The posters would be displayed next to each other in elementry and middle schools, and would hopefully garner the attention of the students walking by.

This is the first in a set of two posters designed to be displayed in a school hallway or a similar environment. It features a bold, hand-drawn aesthetic meant to feel playful and approachable, with the goal of appealing directly to children.

The second poster builds on the same hand-drawn, kid-friendly aesthetic but focuses on delivering simple, engaging facts. Its goal is to educate while still feeling fun and visually approachable, helping kids understand why choosing reusable bottles matters.

This mock-up shows both posters displayed together in a school hallway, demonstrating how the designs interact as a cohesive set. The playful, hand-drawn style stands out in the space, making the educational content engaging and visually appealing for students.
